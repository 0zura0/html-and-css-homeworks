Fools and their money

Interview: Rosie Collington

They argue that the consulting industry weakens our businesses, infantilises our governments and warps our economies. Quite a claim given the size and power of consultancies, who apparently have been largely ghosting them in response. The Mint talked to Rosie to understand more about what the problem is and how it might be fixed.

Rosie colingtons is a political economist and writer, exploring public sector reform, outsourcing, climate governance, and the political economy of the state. Co-authored with my PhD supervisor, Mariana Mazzucato, my forthcoming book The Big Con: How the Consulting Industry Weakens our Businesses, Infantilizes our Governments and Warps our Economies will be published in the UK in February 2023 by Penguin/Allen Lane and in the US in March 2023 by Penguin Press. Translations will subsequently be published by Laterza (Italy), Campus Verlag (Germany), Epikentro (Greece), Taurus (Spain), Companhia de Letras (Brazil), Heterodox (Poland), and Nieuw Amsterdam (Netherlands). media writing has been published in the Guardian, the New Statesman and openDemocracy, amongst other places. I regularly provide policy advice to think tanks, trade unions, and other (not-for-profit) organisations.
